## Runtime boundaries

1. The battle engine consumes the real encounter and level data. It does not hard-code one showcase enemy. Party stats derive from saved advancement, and victory rewards return to the same versioned authority.

2. Spirit is now a live combat resource rather than a camp-only field. Skills declare costs or gains, unavailable actions fail closed, and the deterministic policy can fall back to Guard. The six shipped statuses alter Spirit, Pace, Recovery, or activation damage with explicit lifecycle logs.

3. All 18 objective action families now have bespoke presentation metadata and deterministic in-arena tokens. This establishes readable functional presentation; it does not establish that every encounter has final hand-authored cut-ins, effects, or animation polish.

4. Field interactions, hazards, exits, placed encounters, and objective tokens are persistent runtime behavior. The canonical witness proves the current route with 1,428 legal moves and 239 interactions. A deterministic witness proves reachability, not player comprehension or controller feel.

5. Optional-content evidence covers 13 finite quests plus 18 finite witness chronicles. The finite runner acknowledges every one of the 288 authored chronicle lines, completes all 67 stages, uses canonical victory evidence for combat stages, applies 31 rewards once across progression authorities, and refuses replay. Its zero-time receipt is intentionally not duration evidence.

6. The witness fieldwork catalog contains 152 deterministic nodes with a 729-step minimum. The exact-movement runner executes that full minimum through the public field runtime across 67 fresh authored contexts, emits one moved event per step, and reaches every node in order with zero coordinate jumps. It records no elapsed time.

7. First clears remain manual. Auto-Grind is available only on repeats and changes the presentation schedule for intro, movement, commands, enemy turns, Recovery, result, and reward. Engine decisions and rewards remain invariant across 1x/2x/4x schedules.

8. Cross-page telemetry separates narrative/exploration, first-clear combat, repeat grind, and camp time; hidden, resolved-battle, and 30-second-idle periods do not accrue. Ordinary narrative credits require a same-run receipt with all 60 canonical scenes, the exact eleven-cluster Storyworld decision sequence and its eleven played consequences, at least 300 active minutes, and zero unattributed time. The 215-activity ledger and 1,200-minute requirement remain a separate optional completionist verdict. Credits reconcile receipt and Storyworld authorities before sealing; chapter checkpoints and deterministic runners cannot create duration proof.

9. The core script, 34 Storyworld scenes, companion catalogue, council catalogue, archive, and 60 canonical scene operations are shipped runtime volume.
