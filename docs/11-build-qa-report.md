## Runtime boundaries

1. The battle engine consumes the real encounter and level data. It does not hard-code one showcase enemy. Party stats derive from saved advancement, and victory rewards return to the same versioned authority.

2. Spirit is now a live combat resource rather than a camp-only field. Skills declare costs or gains, unavailable actions fail closed, and the deterministic policy can fall back to Guard. The six shipped statuses alter Spirit, Pace, Recovery, or activation damage with explicit lifecycle logs.

3. All 18 objective action families now have bespoke presentation metadata and deterministic in-arena tokens. This establishes readable functional presentation; it does not establish that every encounter has final hand-authored cut-ins, effects, or animation polish.

4. Field interactions, hazards, exits, placed encounters, and objective tokens are persistent runtime behavior. The canonical witness proves the current route with 1,419 legal moves and 236 interactions. A deterministic witness proves reachability, not player comprehension or controller feel.

5. Optional-content evidence covers 13 finite quests plus 18 finite witness chronicles. The finite runner acknowledges every one of the 288 authored chronicle lines, completes all 67 stages, uses canonical victory evidence for combat stages, applies 31 rewards once across progression authorities, and refuses replay. Its zero-time receipt is intentionally not duration evidence.

6. The witness fieldwork catalog contains 152 deterministic nodes with a 729-step minimum. The exact-movement runner executes that full minimum through the public field runtime across 67 fresh authored contexts, emits one moved event per step, and reaches every node in order with zero coordinate jumps. It records no elapsed time.

7. First clears remain manual. Auto-Grind is available only on repeats and changes the presentation schedule for intro, movement, commands, enemy turns, Recovery, result, and reward. Engine decisions and rewards remain invariant across 1x/2x/4x schedules.

8. Cross-page telemetry separates narrative/exploration, first-clear combat, repeat grind, and camp time; hidden, resolved-battle, and 30-second-idle periods do not accrue. Story completion does not seal the receipt. The player-facing ending requires all 215 route activities, then explicit credits completion; duration proof additionally requires the same clean-start receipt, all 60 beats, all 23 first clears, and at least 1,200 active minutes. The Credits export records category/chapter time, route totals and IDs, receipt revision, and a fail-closed combined verdict; its three run-bound narrative ledgers must match the receipt UUID and attributed chapter time must equal total time. Chapter checkpoints help locate pacing shortfalls but cannot satisfy proof. Deterministic runners record zero time.

9. The 37,718-word core script, 83,435-word companion catalogue, 27,506-word council catalogue, 31,163-word archive, and 60 scene operations are shipped runtime volume. The all-finite canonical-choice path exposes 76,547 companion words and 25,072 council words. Reading-speed and activity formulas estimate the intended route at 776.805/1,232.141/1,918.255 minutes low/reference/high. At reference assumptions it is 1,231.834 minutes with 2× repeat presentation and 1,231.681 with 4× because only four repeat schedules accelerate. Only a same-run human receipt can prove actual duration.

10. The browser now uses deterministic editable art for all 19 combat boards plus party field/combat/portrait, field terrain, regular-enemy, primary-boss, delivery/essence VFX, six-status lifecycle, four-role NPC, and 25-item atlases. Leader selection, party walk-B, terrain overlays, typed field characters, telegraph evasion, and defeat feedback remain outside movement/collision/settlement/proof timing authority; exact metadata prevents collective talks and props from receiving one-person art. Isolated Chrome requires the new atlases to reach `ready`; source/unit gates own exact mappings and fallback exclusions. The segmented no-state-injection route belongs to recorded runtime `d45dfb2` and predates these presentation waves. Subjective responsive/readability and visual comparison, audio review, input feel, Narrator/NVDA testing, external cultural review, and a human full-route playthrough remain outstanding.
